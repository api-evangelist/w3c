                    <p>In late March W3C organized its third "<a href="https://www.w3.org/calendar/breakouts-day-2026/">Breakouts Day</a>," a (fully remote) information sharing opportunity for the W3C community. Breakout sessions provide W3C participants with a chance to share brand new ideas for the World Wide Web or gather new perspectives for handling thorny problems.</p><p>This year people proposed <strong>16 sessions</strong>, which we group here by themes (picked arbitrarily by us, and in no particular order):</p><dl><dt><p>Policy-related</p></dt><dd><ul><li>Age-based restrictions on the web: advancing the discussion</li><li><abbr title="Internet Society">ISOC</abbr> Policymakers Program: A model for supporting one interoperable world-wide web?</li><li>Proposal for foundational structural re-alignment of EN 304-617 in the context of the web security model and <abbr title="Cyber Resilience Act">CRA</abbr></li></ul></dd><dt><p>How the community gets work done</p></dt><dd><ul><li>AI-generated software and web standardization</li><li>Breaking out of silos: Seeing cross-Layer coordination failures on the web</li><li>Structuring design choices with an interactive decision tool</li><li>Two editors for HTML slides</li></ul></dd><dt><p>Horizontal topics</p></dt><dd><ul><li>Improving the conformance model - Web Sustainability edition</li><li>Cognitive accessibility research modules: Voice systems, wayfinding, safety (algorithms and data), and supported decision-making</li></ul></dd><dt><p>Web technology</p></dt><dd><ul><li>Pointer Events - Gesture support</li><li>From hyper-layering to practical SVGMap: AppLayers &amp; community updates</li><li>Support for HEIF as an imagery format in web browsers</li><li>Originator Profile in practice: A year of deployments</li><li>Applying trust, scale, and usability factors to standardize voice agent web integration</li><li>Reviving the ActivityPub Social API</li><li>Light web-based apps: from chatbot's interactions to MiniApps</li></ul></dd></dl><p>Over 200 people participated in these sessions, and we noted especially strong participation for "Age-based restrictions on the web," "AI-generated software and web standardization," and "Support for HEIF as an imagery format."</p><p>The <a href="https://www.w3.org/calendar/breakouts-day-2026/">Breakouts Day 2026 calendar</a> lists the sessions, and for each session there is a link to more details about that session. The details include links to agendas, minutes, slides, and any recordings of presentations (where available). For questions about the topics or other follow-up discussion, please visit the <a href="https://github.com/w3c/breakouts-day-2026/issues">GitHub repository</a> where the sessions were proposed.</p>
