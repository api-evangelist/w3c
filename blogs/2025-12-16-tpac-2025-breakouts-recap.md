<div class="component component--text">
                    <p>Breakout sessions are one of the most popular activities during TPAC. Participants organize discussions about urgent, exciting and important topics related to Web technology, W3C and the future of the Web.</p><p>As part of <a href="https://www.w3.org/2025/11/TPAC/">TPAC 2025</a>, participants proposed and ran <a href="https://github.com/w3c/tpac2025-breakouts/issues">85 breakout sessions</a> for the more than 500 participants on site in Kobe, Japan, and nearly 200 remote participants.</p><p>A handful of themes stood out this year. The following groups are informal, but give a sense of the range of community-driven topics:</p><ul><li>AI<ul><li>AI Agents and The Web</li><li>WebSpatial API for Spatialized HTML/CSS and Spatialized PWAs on spatial and multimodal AI devices</li><li>Agentic Browsing and the Web's Security Model</li><li>Design and Implementation of the Agent Network Protocol (ANP)</li><li>Semantics for the Agentic Web</li><li>Stronger Together: Super-charging Agentic AI with Accessibility Destinations</li><li>AI Driven Web Standards Specification</li></ul></li><li>Accessibility<ul><li>Accessibility Roles and Responsibilities Mapping (ARRM)</li><li>Accessibility Compat Data Update</li><li>Effective and trustworthy conformance evaluation for WCAG and beyond</li><li>The Future of Assistive Tech Interoperability with ARIA-AT</li><li>WCAG2Mobile: presentation, explanation, feedback</li><li>WCAG 2.2 and non-Latin languages</li><li>Dynamic Accessibility Remediation</li><li>WCAG 3 update</li><li>Accessibility Maturity Model Group Note Update</li></ul></li><li>Identity, Credentials, and Wallets<ul><li>Converged Use Cases for the Credential Management API</li><li>Web platform features to enable biometric liveness detection</li><li>Mobile student ID implementation into Japanese society.</li><li>Digital Identity Wallet Project in Taiwan: Entering the Second Year</li><li>Mitigate Threats for Digital Credentials API: Episode III - Revenge of the Wallet</li><li>Identity Systems and Threats: Towards a Holistic View</li><li>Harm and Threat Modeling for National Digital Identities</li><li>A Lean4-Verified Protocol for Smart Cities Addressing the Three Core Challenges of DIDs and VCs</li><li>Age-Based Restrictions Have Come for the Web</li></ul></li><li>Privacy and Security<ul><li>Privacy Pass for digital ads verification</li><li>Geolocation and Privacy</li><li>Private Cross-Origin/Site Prefetch</li><li>Security guidance for web developers</li><li>W3C Threat Modeling Guide and the Threat Model for the Web</li><li>New approach to solving client-side security</li><li>Payment Authentication on the Web: an Issuer’s Perspective</li><li>W3C as an organization / governance</li><li>Future of the Open Web</li><li>Navigating the Future</li><li>Tell Us What's Wrong with the Process</li><li>Involving students and academia in web platform implementation</li><li>Restarting W3C QA - Quality Assurance</li><li>For Everyone: Towards a Sustainable Future for Independent Standards Work</li><li>Enabling Effective AC Participation</li></ul></li></ul><p>Each <a href="https://github.com/w3c/tpac2025-breakouts/issues">issue in the GitHub repo</a> describes a session and includes (where known) links to agendas, slides, minutes, and any recorded presentations. We’ve asked each session organizer to communicate where they expect discussion to continue.</p><p>As in previous years, attendees have provided positive feedback on the sessions themselves and how we organized the agenda. This year for the first time we scheduled breakouts on multiple days, which reduced the number of parallel sessions in a given time slot (and thus reduced frustration when people wanted to attend “competing” sessions).&nbsp;</p><blockquote class="component component--quote"><p>All in all, there were 9 sessions with a maximum of 10 rooms in parallel this year, to be compared with 6 sessions with a maximum of 14 rooms in parallel in 2024, for the same number of sessions (85 in both cases).&nbsp;</p></blockquote><p>This seems to have helped because we’ve heard more expressions of satisfaction and fewer laments. Given the feedback, we have already started thinking about how to add more breakouts next year on more days to further reduce the number of sessions at the same time.</p><p>As a reminder, anyone may attend TPAC breakout sessions remotely at no cost, so please join us for <a href="https://www.w3.org/events/tpac/2026/tpac-2026/">TPAC 2026</a> breakouts next year in October 2026.</p>
